Dear Carlos,
I really hope we can count on your generous help now. If you remember, we have an urgent need in our dorms where we house some of our middle and high school students throughout the week.
They live far away from campus, so we are truly their home away from home. We want our Native American children to feel as secure as possible-- especially since some of them come from tumultuous home environments where poverty and despair weigh heavily on them.
They love having their own place where they can stretch out and relax after a day of classes. But right now, our students’ bunk beds are in pretty bad shape. In fact, since they are 10 years old and made out of wood, they have become very worn and loose.
We need to replace them with safer, more durable bunk beds that are made out of steel.And when you give by November 14, you'll help cover the $30,000 cost of replacing 43 dorm bunk beds.
Please give nowand help our girls and boys have a secure place to rest their heads at night. You’ll also provide for their other educational expenses, including keeping our classrooms safe and warm, and making sure the children have nutritious food.
Thanks for all you do for our kids,
Curtis Yarlott
